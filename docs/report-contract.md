# Report Contract

This document defines the contract for the client-facing report generated by TCRIA.

The report is not a dump of technical output. It is the main product deliverable.

Reference artifacts:

- template: [report-template.md](report-template.md)
- example markdown: [report-example.md](report-example.md)
- example pdf: [../output/pdf/tcria-compliance-report-example.pdf](../output/pdf/tcria-compliance-report-example.pdf)

## Main Rule

The client-facing report must be understandable on first reading.

When a client opens the document, the structure should immediately show:

- what was analyzed;
- how the material was read when that matters for reliability;
- what the main compliance issues are;
- what evidence supports them;
- what is still uncertain;
- what should be prioritized next.

## Format Independence

The report contract applies regardless of final surface:

- PDF;
- HTML;
- Markdown;
- other formatted document outputs.

The delivery format may vary, but the information architecture must remain consistent.

## Mandatory Presentation Standard

The client-facing report must:

- be formatted as a document, not as a log;
- use clear section hierarchy;
- highlight the most important findings early;
- keep each finding visually separable from the next;
- connect each conclusion to supporting evidence;
- expose limitations without hiding them in annexes.

## Mandatory Report Sections

Every client-facing report must contain, at minimum:

1. report identification;
2. executive summary;
3. analysis scope;
4. reading coverage and extraction provenance;
5. applied review rules;
6. main compliance findings;
7. supporting evidence references;
8. unresolved points or limitations;
9. next review priorities;
10. annexes or technical appendices when needed.

## First-Page Requirement

The opening section of the report must let the client understand, without reading the full document:

- the analyzed scope;
- the overall compliance situation found in the batch;
- whether part of the batch depended on OCR or remained unreadable;
- the highest-priority issues;
- the recommended next actions.

## Finding Block Standard

Each major finding should be presented in a consistent block with fields such as:

- finding title;
- compliance area or rule involved;
- severity or risk level;
- conformity status;
- explanation of the issue;
- supporting evidence;
- business or operational impact;
- recommended follow-up.

## Forbidden Client-Facing Patterns

The client-facing report must not:

- look like raw OCR output;
- look like an internal debug trace;
- mix evidence and conclusion without labels;
- bury the main issue under long technical text;
- force the reader to infer what matters by themselves.

## Output Responsibility

The report must give an account of the system's work.

At minimum, it must explain:

- what the system reviewed;
- how the system obtained the readable text;
- what the system concluded;
- why it concluded that;
- what the system could not conclude safely.

## Failure Condition

The output is considered failed if the information exists internally but the client still cannot quickly understand the result after opening the document.
