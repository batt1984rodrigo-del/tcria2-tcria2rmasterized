# TCRIA Client Pilot Guide

## Purpose

The TCRIA client pilot demonstrates how a bounded document batch can be transformed into an evidence-linked compliance review and a clear decision recommendation.

The pilot is intended to answer one defined question, such as:

> Based on the submitted evidence, should this supplier, process, contract or control be approved, approved with conditions, held for review or not continued yet?

## What the client submits

The client provides:

- one bounded and authorized document batch;
- a document inventory when available;
- the review question;
- the relevant policy, checklist or decision criteria when available;
- a responsible contact for clarifications and final validation.

Documents should be fictional, sanitized or submitted under an agreed confidentiality and data-processing arrangement.

## Pilot workflow

1. **Scope confirmation** — define the batch and the question to be answered.
2. **Controlled intake** — register the files received and identify unreadable or unsupported material.
3. **Reading** — use direct extraction where possible and OCR fallback where necessary.
4. **Evidence organization** — separate confirmed facts, missing evidence, inconsistencies and reading limitations.
5. **Compliance review** — compare the evidence against the supplied criteria and the defined scope.
6. **Decision recommendation** — state the recommended course of action, rationale, conditions and escalation points.
7. **Human validation** — the client's responsible professional reviews the report and makes the final institutional decision.

## Decision recommendation categories

### PROCEED

The submitted batch contains sufficient, consistent and current evidence for continuation within the defined scope.

### PROCEED WITH CONDITIONS

Continuation is supported, but specific corrective actions, updated documents or confirmations are required.

### HOLD FOR REVIEW

A responsible professional must review material inconsistencies, ambiguity or insufficient evidence before continuation.

### DO NOT PROCEED YET

Critical evidence is missing, invalid or materially inconsistent. The recommendation applies only to the submitted batch and may change after remediation.

## Pilot deliverables

The pilot produces:

- document inventory and scope summary;
- reading status and OCR provenance;
- confirmed facts;
- unresolved items and evidence gaps;
- findings linked to document references;
- decision recommendation and rationale;
- required conditions or corrective actions;
- limitations and human-review points;
- next review priorities;
- one structured, client-facing report.

## What the pilot does not do

The pilot does not:

- inspect the client's entire organization or systems;
- guarantee that the submitted batch is complete;
- replace legal advice, audit opinion or responsible management judgment;
- issue sanctions, accusations or final institutional decisions;
- infer a critical fact without evidence and present it as confirmed;
- silently discard unreadable or imperfect documents.

## Suggested first pilot

A practical first pilot is a supplier qualification review with 10 to 30 fictional or sanitized documents.

Suggested review question:

> Should the supplier be approved, approved with conditions, held for review or not approved yet based exclusively on this document batch?

Suggested evidence:

- company registration;
- tax clearance;
- insurance certificate;
- integrity or conflict declaration;
- beneficial ownership declaration;
- technical qualification evidence;
- internal approval checklist.

## Success criteria

The pilot is successful when a non-technical responsible professional can:

- understand the recommended decision;
- trace each material conclusion to evidence;
- distinguish confirmed facts from missing information;
- see which documents were read through OCR;
- identify the limits of the analysis;
- know which action should be taken next;
- retain authority over the final decision.

## Client protection principle

TCRIA must never present an automated recommendation as an unquestionable decision. Its value is not only the recommendation, but the visible reasoning, evidence trail, uncertainty and conditions that allow a responsible person to review it.
